# CITATIONS.md
## Small Business Website + SEO + Google Business Agency

**Applies to:** every product type (1–5). Citations are a **cross-type production-cutover deliverable** per `TECH.md` §1. Every client launches with a citation baseline before `noindex` is flipped off.

**Scope:** local citations / business-directory listings outside of the website itself. The website's GBP optimization rules live in `SEO.md` §8; `CITATIONS.md` owns the wider directory landscape.

**Audience:** Berlin-based agency, primarily DE-market clients. PT + BR get short seed lists in §5 and §6.

---

## Rules at a glance

- Citations in 2026 are **hygiene + AI-search input**, not a top-5 local-ranking factor. Build a clean baseline at launch; don't pay for monthly maintenance below €500/mo retainer (per Sterling Sky 2026 retainer-experiments evidence).
- **Industry-specific citations matter more than generic ones.** Jameda for doctors, Tripadvisor for restaurants, Treatwell for beauty still drive **direct traffic + bookings** independent of any Google-ranking effect.
- **NAP consistency is mandatory.** Same name, same street, same phone, same website canonical across every claimed listing. A single mismatch erodes Google's entity confidence and confuses customers.
- **Per-directory premium-upsell traps are real.** Gelbe Seiten's Sellwerk pitch, 11880's ProfiEintrag telesales, Das Örtliche's premium tier — each operates differently. Document in the retainer agreement before claiming, so the client knows what to refuse.
- **Aggregator subscriptions (Yext, Uberall, Moz Local) are NOT ROI-positive** for single-location Berlin SMEs below €500/mo retainer. Default: per-directory manual claim at launch (3-4 hrs one-time) + optional BrightLocal one-off (~€200) for the long tail.
- **Canonical NAP declared per client in `BRIEF.md`** before any directory is claimed. The BRIEF.md NAP is the single source of truth every listing matches.

## 1. State of citations in 2026 (honest framing)

The honest read: **citations are a hygiene item with a renewed AI-search tailwind, not a top-5 local-ranking lever.** Worth doing at launch; not worth paying monthly for once the baseline exists.

**What the 2026 research actually says:**

- **[Whitespark Local Search Ranking Factors 2026](https://whitespark.ca/local-search-ranking-factors/)** still ranks citation signals in the **top 8** for Local Pack and Local Finder, but reputation, on-page, and behavioural signals all weigh heavier. The new wrinkle: **3 of the top 5 "AI Search Visibility" factors are citations or third-party mentions** (presence on "best of" lists, prominence on industry-relevant domains, authority of unstructured citations). LLM-driven local discovery (ChatGPT search, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) re-reads directory + listicle content as grounding data.
- **[BrightLocal 2026 survey](https://www.brightlocal.com/learn/google-local-algorithm-and-ranking-factors/)** ranks citations **#6 for Local Pack** and **tied 4th for Local Organic**. Steady vs. 2023. Median top-10 local business has ~81 citations.
- **[Sterling Sky retainer experiments](https://www.sterlingsky.ca/should-seo-company-build-citations-every-month/)** show **continuous monthly citation building rarely moves rankings** once a baseline of ~30-50 indexed citations exists. The exception: **industry-specific directories** (Jameda for doctors, Avvo/Justia for lawyers, Tripadvisor for hospitality) — those still move the needle.

**Agency posture (load-bearing rule):**

> Treat the **universal + DE-general + 1-3 vertical-specific** list as a **one-time setup at launch** (~3-4 hours per client). Skip the "submit to 200 directories" packages — they're noise that compounds maintenance cost without moving rankings.

**Where consensus is unsettled:**

Whether NAP *inconsistency* (not absence) actively suppresses rankings. Whitespark and BrightLocal say yes (the often-cited "40% less likely to appear in pack" stat); Sterling Sky tests suggest Google de-duplicates competently and inconsistencies mostly matter for user trust, not algorithm. **Safe default: treat consistency as mandatory regardless of which side is right** — there's no scenario where inconsistency helps.

**The "are citations dead" question — vertical-dependent verdict:**

- **Doctors / lawyers / hotels / restaurants:** industry-specific citations (Jameda, Anwalt.de, Tripadvisor, Booking.com) **still drive direct traffic and bookings** independent of any ranking effect. Non-negotiable — claim and maintain.
- **Generic plumber / retail / pet groomer:** ~30 indexed citations across the universal + DE-general stack is enough. Beyond that, ROI is ≈ zero. Don't pay for citation-building services at this end of the catalog.
- **AI-search tailwind:** LLMs re-read **high-authority directory + listicle content** (Yelp, Tripadvisor, Jameda, "best of Berlin" articles) as grounding data. The long-tail submitter directories don't appear in LLM training data. Focus the citation budget on high-authority, not breadth.

## 3. DE general directories + per-directory trap table

DE-market clients claim these on top of the universal stack. ~1-2 hours total at launch.

⚠️ **Three of these directories run premium-upsell traps that surprise unprepared owners.** Document each trap in the retainer agreement BEFORE claiming so the owner knows what sales call to refuse.

| Directory | Free listing | Premium-upsell trap | Cancellation mechanic |
|---|---|---|---|
| **[Gelbe Seiten](https://www.gelbeseiten.de/starteintrag)** | ✅ Always free (basic listing) | **Sellwerk premium upsell** via dedicated sales reps (~€29.90/mo). Sellwerk is Gelbe Seiten's reseller arm (operated by Ströer Digital Publishing). The free 3-month "premium trial" auto-renews if not cancelled. | Cancel with **Sellwerk** (not Gelbe Seiten) — requires written notice **2 weeks before** trial end. Calendar-reminder the date at claim time. |
| **[Das Örtliche](https://www.dasoertliche.de/)** | ✅ Always free (basic listing) | Das Örtliche premium tier (~€19.90/mo) — distinct product from Gelbe Seiten with different pricing. | Written notice required. Distinct from Sellwerk cancellation — do not confuse. |
| **[Das Telefonbuch](https://www.dastelefonbuch.de/)** | ✅ Always free | Bundled with Gelbe Seiten / Das Örtliche via Sellwerk — same trap mechanic. | Bundled cancellation via Sellwerk. |
| **[11880.com](https://www.11880.com/)** | ✅ Always free | **"ProfiEintrag" paid placement** — aggressive telesales follow-up within ~2 weeks of free claim. Sales calls are persistent and high-pressure; some owners pay just to make the calls stop. | **Do not answer unknown numbers for 2 weeks after claiming.** No formal cancellation; just don't engage. |
| **[meinestadt.de](https://eintragen.meinestadt.de/)** | ✅ Always free (especially strong for Berlin — city portal weight) | Paid advertising upsell — **no auto-renewal on basic listing**. Safe for the agency's default scope. | N/A — pure free listing. |
| **[berlin.de](https://www.berlin.de/)** | ✅ Always free | None. Run by the Berliner Senatsverwaltung; government-adjacent trust signal. | N/A |
| **[Cylex Deutschland](https://www.cylex.de/)** | ✅ Free | None significant. Lower value than the above. | N/A |
| **[GoYellow](https://www.goyellow.de/)** | ✅ Free | None significant. Even lower value. | N/A |

**Berlin-specific note:** `berlin.de` is the most under-claimed entry in this list. It's a government-adjacent portal — claiming a free business listing there is a real differentiation move that most agencies skip. Always claim for Berlin-primary clients.

**Sellwerk handling protocol (retainer rule):**

When the agency manages claims on the client's behalf:

1. Claim the free basic listing in the owner's name (never the agency's name — the listing belongs to the client).
2. Calendar-reminder the Sellwerk cancellation deadline (2 weeks before the 3-month trial end).
3. Document in the retainer agreement: "Agency claims the free basic listing; Sellwerk premium upsell is a separate Sellwerk-Inhaber commercial decision and is not included in the retainer."
4. If the owner accepts the Sellwerk upsell, the agency does not pay it from the retainer — it's a direct client-to-Sellwerk arrangement.

## 7. NAP canonical template (declared per client in BRIEF.md)

**Rule:** every client's BRIEF.md carries a canonical NAP block at the top, owner-confirmed before scaffold starts. Every directory listing matches this block exactly.

**Template:**

```text
## Canonical NAP

**Name:**     [Exact legal name — no city, no keyword stuffing]
              e.g. "Friseur Schmidt"  — NOT "Friseur Schmidt Berlin Kreuzberg Best Haircut"
**Street:**   [Spelled out: "Bergmannstraße 25" — not "Bergmannstr. 25"]
              Pick ONE form (full or abbreviated) and use everywhere.
**ZIP/City:** "10961 Berlin"
**Country:**  "Germany" / "Deutschland" (match each directory's locale field)
**Phone:**    +49 30 1234567   (E.164 international, with spaces for readability)
              Avoid "030 / 12 34 56 7" on directories — keep that format for print only.
**Website:**  https://example.de/  (with https, with trailing slash, no www unless www is canonical)

**Owner-confirmed:** [date]
**Last NAP-consistency audit:** [date] (semi-annual per CITATIONS.md §9)
```

### Tolerable variance (no fix required)

- `Straße` vs `Str.` — Google deduplicates these.
- Phone formatting differences (E.164 vs national) — as long as the digits match.
- `https://` vs `http://` when the site auto-redirects.
- Display of address with vs without country line (varies per directory's form).

### Killing inconsistencies (must fix)

- Different street address (wrong building number; missing Hinterhof / HH designation).
- Different phone number entirely (e.g., owner's mobile on one listing, landline on another). Pick one and use everywhere; secondary numbers go in "additional phone" fields only.
- Different business name (legal name vs trade name vs "DBA" vs marketing variant).
- Different domain (www vs apex when one returns 404).

**Audit pattern:** every 6 months (per §9), run a manual NAP audit across the universal + DE-general + vertical-specific lists for the client. Flag any of the killing inconsistencies as 🔴; fix immediately.

**Sources:** [SEO-DAY NAP consistency guide](https://www.seo-day.de/wiki/local-seo/citations/nap-konsistenz.php?lang=en), [BrightLocal NAP fundamentals](https://www.brightlocal.com/learn/what-is-nap/), [Birdeye NAP formatting](https://birdeye.com/blog/local-seo-format-nap/).

## 8. Aggregator verdict — Yext / Uberall / BrightLocal / Whitespark

**Default: do not subscribe.** The agency's clients (single-location Berlin SMEs at €200-500/mo retainer) do not get ROI from a paid aggregator. Per-directory manual claim at launch covers the universal + DE-general + 1-3 vertical-specific lists in ~3-4 hours.

| Service | Cost | Coverage | Verdict for our segment |
|---|---|---|---|
| **[Yext](https://www.yext.com/)** | ~$199-999/location/year | 50+ DE pubs including Gelbe Seiten, 11880, Das Örtliche, Apple, plus deep US/EU breadth | ❌ Not ROI-positive below €500/mo retainer. Made for multi-location chains, not single-location SMEs. |
| **[Uberall](https://uberall.com/)** | Enterprise-priced (quote-only) | Berlin-HQ; was hibu/Navads. EU-native, GDPR-tight, broader DE-directory reach than Yext. | ❌ Not realistic at our retainer scope. |
| **[BrightLocal Citation Builder](https://www.brightlocal.com/learn/citation-building/)** | ~$2/citation one-off; ~€200 for ~30 manual citations including duplicate cleanup | Manual submissions to a curated set | ⚠️ **Acceptable as a one-time push** for retainer clients at €300+/mo. Not recurring. |
| **[Whitespark Citation Building](https://whitespark.ca/services/citation-building/)** | $110-250 per package (one-off) | Manual submissions, strongest reputation in the niche, humans check each entry | ⚠️ Same role as BrightLocal — one-off use when the long tail matters. |

**Recommendation for retainer scope:**

- **≤ €300/mo retainer:** universal + DE-general manual claim at launch only. No paid services.
- **€300-500/mo retainer:** universal + DE-general + 1-3 vertical-specific at launch. Optional BrightLocal one-off (~€200, pass-through to client per `SEO.md` §8.4.8 cost pass-through rule) at month 3 if the long tail matters for the vertical.
- **€500+/mo retainer:** as above + 6-month BrightLocal cleanup audit (re-run their tool against current state, fix what's drifted).

**Document the pass-through arrangement in the retainer contract** before purchasing any of these — surprise tool costs surfaced mid-quarter cause friction.

## 9. 6-month refresh cadence (retainer clients)

**Citations are not a monthly deliverable.** Once the baseline is built at launch, the maintenance load drops to a **semi-annual audit** for retainer clients. Per `SALES.md` §10.

### What the 6-month audit covers

1. **NAP-consistency re-verification.** Manually check the canonical NAP from `BRIEF.md` against each claimed listing in universal + DE-general + vertical-specific. Flag any of §7 "killing inconsistencies" as 🔴; fix immediately.
2. **Hours + holiday-schedule propagation.** If the client updated hours on GBP, propagate to listings that support manual hours (Yelp, Tripadvisor, Treatwell, etc.). Many directories pull from a stale cache; manual nudge needed.
3. **Photo refresh propagation.** New hero photo on GBP → propagate to directories that support a primary image (Yelp, Tripadvisor, Treatwell).
4. **Premium-upsell audit.** Confirm no listings have silently flipped to a paid tier (Sellwerk cancellation deadline missed, 11880 ProfiEintrag accepted in a moment of weakness, etc.). If found, decide: keep paid (document in retainer), or unwind.
5. **AI-search visibility check** (€500+/mo retainer): query the client's primary keyword in ChatGPT / Perplexity / Google AI Overviews. If the business is cited, note the source directory; if not, identify which authoritative third-party mention is missing and add it to a "next 6-month sprint" list.

### Why semi-annual and not monthly

[Sterling Sky's 2025 retainer experiments](https://www.sterlingsky.ca/should-seo-company-build-citations-every-month/) show **monthly citation maintenance rarely moves rankings** once a ~30-50 baseline exists. Frequency doesn't help; what helps is:

- Catching the rare killing inconsistency before Google de-prioritizes the business
- Catching the rare upsell-trap flip before it eats the retainer margin
- Refreshing the assets that the directories cache (photos, hours, holiday schedules)

Semi-annual hits all three without the time-tax of monthly churn.

### Per-tier deliverable

| Retainer tier | 6-month audit deliverable | Effort |
|---|---|---|
| ≤ €300/mo | Audit summary + flag killing inconsistencies for owner to fix | ~30 min |
| €300-500/mo | Audit + agency fixes any killing inconsistencies + propagates hours/photo updates to top 5 directories | ~1 hr |
| €500+/mo | Above + AI-search visibility check + 6-month-sprint plan for next half | ~2 hrs |
