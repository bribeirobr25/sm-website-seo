## Businesses surveyed

### 1. Eispatisserie Hokey Pokey

- **URL:** https://www.hokey-pokey.de/
- **Locations surveyed:** Stargarder Straße 73, 10437 Prenzlauer Berg (original) + Mitte takeaway boutique
- **Retrieval date:** 2026-05-20
- **Profile:** Premium ice-cream patisserie, founded 2011 by pastry chef Niko Robert (ex-Ritz Carlton Berlin). Multiple locations now; flagship in Prenzlauer Berg. Patisserie-precision approach to gelato; international ingredient sourcing (Valrhona chocolate, Sicilian pistachios, Bronte, Tahitian vanilla).
- **Voice / register patterns:**
  - **Du-form throughout** (`Wir freuen uns auf Euch · Such dir einfach deinen Wunschladen`) — premium positioning still uses informal address; this is the Berlin-Prenzlauer-Berg norm
  - "Seit 2011" — heritage anchor placed prominently
  - Quality-via-specificity (producer + region + varietal) instead of adjective stacking: `Piemonteser Haselnusscreme` · `sizilianische Pistazien` · `Valrhona Guanaja Sorbet`
  - Playful irreverence beneath the premium veneer: `Von allem nur das Beste – und davon eigentlich viel zu viel`
  - Emoji-friendly in social/secondary copy (🎉 🥰) — kept off the homepage hero
  - Exclamation marks acceptable for warmth
- **Anti-patterns to avoid:** None observed — site demonstrates the gastronomy template §Copy voice rules well
- **Place-identity cues:** Prenzlauer Berg location worn explicitly; nav says `Unsere Läden` (multi-location framing); Berlin neighborhood-pillar tone over destination-restaurant tone
- **What's distinctive:** The patisserie-craft framing — every flavor is positioned as a pastry-chef creation (cake-grade ingredients in gelato form). This is a sub-archetype between traditional gelateria and dessert atelier; not what we're going for.

### 2. Cuore di Vetro

- **URL:** https://www.cuoredivetro.berlin/
- **Locations surveyed:** Max-Beerstr. 33, 10119 Mitte (flagship) + Friedrichshain location
- **Retrieval date:** 2026-05-20
- **Profile:** Italian gelateria opened 2013 (per `berlinfoodstories.com`; website doesn't state founding year directly). Named after Werner Herzog's 1976 film *Herz aus Glas* / *Cuore di Vetro* — the "glass" metaphor extends to the literal `gläserne Manufaktur` (glass workshop) visible to customers. Mentors cited: Italian gelato masters Giacomo Schiavon, Antonio Mezzalira, Alberto Vitaro.
- **Voice / register patterns:**
  - **Du-form throughout** (`Hier kreieren wir · Ihr auch von früh bis spät · könnt Ihr auch sehen`)
  - "Espresso-Prinzip" — continuous-production-throughout-day philosophy stated as positioning
  - Italian loanwords used sparingly but consistently: `Gelato · Sorbeto · Le Creme · Sorbetti vegani · Coppe di Gelato · Bacio · Nocciola`
  - Nav uses `Ciao` as a Hi-equivalent landing entry — playful Italian touch without committing to full Italian
  - Concise ingredient-focused flavor descriptions (1–3 lines): `Wertvolle Magerkakao, Haselnuss aus Piemonte` for Bacio
  - Cinema reference woven into the brand — distinctive signal of culture-aware positioning
- **Anti-patterns to avoid:** None observed. The cinema-reference brand naming is distinctive but specific to this business — don't copy
- **Place-identity cues:** Mitte address worn explicitly; emphasis on `gläserne Manufaktur` — workshop transparency as differentiation
- **What's distinctive:** The "glass workshop" concept + cinema reference. Aspirational but very specific to their identity.

### 3. Eiscafé Annamaria

- **URL:** https://eiscafe-annamaria.de/
- **Profile:** Classic neighborhood gelateria (Wilmersdorf area). No explicit founding year stated. Plays the "leisure / lingering" positioning rather than the heritage-Italian-family one. Seasonal menu (winter pivots to cakes + waffles + winter drinks).
- **Voice / register patterns:**
  - **Du-form / Ihr-plural** (`Ihr seid herzlich eingeladen · Ihr könnt auch ganze Eistorten bestellen`) — even warmer than the Hokey Pokey du-form, leans toward `Ihr` (plural-friendly) signaling the family-day gathering aspect
  - Voice qualities: `mit Sorgfalt selbst her` (carefully made ourselves) · `ein Ort, an dem man gern verweilt und es sich gutgehen läßt`
  - Heritage signaled through register + craft phrasing rather than year (note: this is a weaker positioning than year-anchored heritage — confirms why "seit 1987" matters for our Eiscafé Bellini choice)
  - **German-only** (no English switcher, no Italian beyond `Eiscafé` itself)
  - Minimal navigation — Speisekarte (PDF) + Eistorten + Impressum + Datenschutz
- **Anti-patterns to avoid:** PDF-only menu is bad practice per `templates/gastronomy.md` §Anti-patterns (menu should be HTML for SEO + i18n + mobile). German-only signals the audience choice but limits Prenzlauer Berg / international-traffic appeal.
- **Place-identity cues:** `auf einer der schönsten Straßen` — implicit Wilmersdorf café-street pride
- **What's distinctive:** Seasonal-menu approach (winter pivot to cakes/waffles/drinks) — interesting but adds operational complexity our demo doesn't need to copy.

### 4. Caffè e Gelato

- **URL:** https://caffe-e-gelato.de/
- **Profile:** Italian gelato + coffee specialist, "seit 1998" — the longest-running of the surveyed group. Positions gelato + espresso as parallel sensory offerings, not coffee-as-side-to-ice-cream.
- **Voice / register patterns:**
  - **Sie-form** dominant — formal register matches the premium-experiential positioning
  - "Die Qualität echter italienischer Eiscreme, die seit 1998 von Hand hergestellt wird" — the canonical "since [year]" + "echter italienischer" + "von Hand" combo all in the hero. Very strong heritage signal.
  - Five-values structure: Design · Geselligkeit · Handwerk · Verkostung · Perfektion
  - Italian regional anchors used at expert level: `Nocciola Tonda Gentile Trilobata del Piemonte` (the specific cultivar, not just "Piemonteser Haselnuss")
  - **Three-language switcher** (Deu / Eng / Ita) — strongest multilingual commitment of the group
  - Sociability framing: `Ein Genuss im Zentrum Berlins, der geteilt werden muss` (a pleasure that must be shared)
- **Anti-patterns to avoid:** Sie-form is a bigger commitment than our archetype needs; we'll use Du like the others. Five-values block is template-y if not done very precisely — skip.
- **Place-identity cues:** `im Zentrum Berlins` — vague, not Bezirk-specific. We'll do better with Prenzlauer Berg explicit.
- **What's distinctive:** The depth of Italian regional sourcing language. We can borrow this pattern (specific producer / cultivar / region naming) without copying their exact wording.

### 5. visitBerlin "Top 11" parlour-survey reference (context)

- **URL:** https://www.visitberlin.de/en/blog/berlins-top-11-ice-cream-parlours
- **Retrieval date:** 2026-05-20
- **What this gave us:** Confirmed several "family/heritage" gelaterias exist in Berlin (Coco Beach, Eiscafé Schober, Aldemir Eis, Eiscafé Annamaria). Confirmed Italian gelaterias use either heritage-since-year framing (Caffè e Gelato, Hokey Pokey) or absence-of-year + craft framing (Annamaria, Cuore di Vetro). Validated that "Prenzlauer Berg + Italian + family + seit-year" is a real archetype — Hokey Pokey occupies the *premium-patisserie* version of that, and there's a clear gap for the *neighborhood-warm-family* version. **That's exactly where Eiscafé Bellini lives.**

## Synthesis

### Register conclusion

**Du-form is correct for Eiscafé Bellini.** 3 of 4 surveyed businesses use Du; the one Sie-form business (Caffè e Gelato) is the most explicitly premium-experiential of the group and the positioning doesn't match our three-generation-family target. Within Du, lean toward **Ihr-plural for invitations + welcoming statements** (`Bei uns seid Ihr immer willkommen`) and **du-singular for direct address** (`Probier unser ...`).

**Heritage anchor:** universal pattern is `seit [Jahr] · von Hand · in Berlin` — three short phrases that together carry decades of trust. For Bellini: `seit 1987 · drei Generationen · in Prenzlauer Berg` is the analogue. The year matters more than the absolute phrasing — Annamaria's no-year approach reads weaker.

**Sentence-length norm:** Short. None of the 4 surveyed businesses run more than 2-3 sentence paragraphs in their hero or about sections. The single sentence is the dominant rhetorical unit.

**Untranslated Italian cues:** ALL surveyed businesses sprinkle Italian — but ONLY for ingredients, regions, product names. The page chrome (nav, buttons, body copy) stays German. Use Italian for: gelato flavor names, ingredient origins (`Piemonte`, `Sicilia`, `Veneto`), Italian dessert names (Bacio, Affogato, Tiramisu). Don't translate into Italian what's already German.

**Bilingual EN presence:** Prenzlauer Berg has heavy international traffic — matches Caffè e Gelato's 3-lang commitment more than Annamaria's German-only. Eiscafé Bellini should ship DE primary + EN secondary, switcher visible.

### Template alignment / contradiction

Maps onto `docs/design/templates/gastronomy.md`:

- **§Copy voice "Quiet, present-tense"** ✓ — confirmed across all 4 surveyed
- **§Copy voice "concrete details build trust faster than adjectives"** ✓ — quality-via-specificity is the dominant pattern (Piemonteser Haselnuss > "high-quality nuts")
- **§Copy voice "no marketing-speak"** ✓ — none of the surveyed businesses use "premium", "exclusive", "revolutionary" register
- **§Anti-patterns "Avoid gradient hero overlays in saturated brand colors"** ✓ — none observed (all 4 use clean photography or solid cream backgrounds)
- **§Anti-patterns "Generic Unsplash stock"** ⚠ — we MUST avoid this in image sourcing (Phase 3). The surveyed businesses use real shop photography, not stock; for our demo we'll select Unsplash images that DON'T read as "stock food photography" cliché (no perfectly-styled overhead-shots-with-flat-lay-props, no model-with-cone-against-pastel-wall — see Phase 3 sourcing brief)

**No contradictions detected** — the template's voice + anti-pattern rules align with the actual Berlin market.

### Owner discussion points

1. **Du vs Ihr balance** — recommend `Ihr` for warm group address (newsletters, hero welcome) and `du` for direct product reference. Single voice owner sign-off (you) needed before Phase 2 copy.
2. **English copy strategy** — recommend EN copy = lightly re-thought, NOT a literal translation. The DE copy uses idioms (`drei Generationen`, `frisch täglich`) that need natural EN equivalents (`three generations`, `freshly made every day` — not the literal `daily fresh`). Owner approves the approach, not each sentence.
3. **Italian sprinkling threshold** — recommend matching the Hokey Pokey level (ingredient names + region names + a few flavor names in Italian; everything else German) rather than the Cuore di Vetro level (Italian nav entry `Ciao`, more Italian flavor descriptions). Owner pick.
4. **Year and family-story specifics** — the "1987 / Treviso / three-generation" narrative is locked from `PORTFOLIO-BUILD-INSTANTIATION-2026-05-20-GASTRONOMY.md` Option A; restate here for confirmation.
